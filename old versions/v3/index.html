Web Design

The M Company

I founded The M Company, a tech startup focused on simplifying and optimizing current mobile and desktop devices to get the most productivity and efficiency out of them. Its name comes from the M theory, which looks to tie all quantum theories together in an elegant manner, just like it hopes to simplify tech.

Taking into account what it stands for and its name, I stripped the logo to the simplest form possible without losing the striking feeling the company was looking for.

At The M Company, I am responsible for taking projects from idea phase to production, as well as taking care of its social media presence and corporate identity







FontDatabase

fontdatabase.app is a web application designed to help designers find alternative fonts. Using an algorithm, it can help you find fonts more (or less) serious, detailed, formal, etc. to the one you input. Users can also help the algorithm grow by suggesting fonts themselves.

fontdatabase.app was featured in Product Hunt and international media outlets such as Periodismo.com, wwwhatsnew, and TekCrispy.

Spur

Spur is a concept mobile app focused on making setting events and reminders easier than ever, implementing speech recognition. The app is straight to the point and so is the website, so as to lead costumers straight to the product.

Vanish

Vanish is a website that guides you through the process of deleting your online information, as well as how to back it up beforehand. Vanish was featured in Product Hunt as well as being a huge success around Twitter.

Evoking the typical striking, overwhelming look of sites of the late 90s/early 2000s, it reminds the visitors why exactly they are leaving social media: information overload.

UI/UX Design


SoleMedia

I started designing and developing SoleMedia based on people's fixation with making the most out of their time using social media. The only apps that are remotely similar focused on content management, rather than timeline aggregation.

Since the time spent on social networks is, on average, quite long, I decided to go for a dark palette, making said time easier on the eye.

M Tool Launcher

I designed The M Tool Launcher starting off the idea of a superapp, and why it never took off. Since people want to have a choice when it comes to each of their phone's features (messaging apps, social media, productivity tools, among other), a single app that does everything will never suit more than a couple individuals.

To solve this, The M Tool Launcher provides the benefits of acting like a superapp: reduces the time spend switching between apps, gives the experience of seamlessness navigation, all without sacrificing user choices.

The M Tool Launcher is currently under development.
