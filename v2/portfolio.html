Corporate Identity


The M Company

The M Company is a tech startup focused on simplifying and optimizing current mobile and desktop devices to get the most productivity and efficiency out of them. Its name comes from the M theory, which looks to tie all quantum theories together in an elegant manner, just like they hope to simplify tech.

Taking into account what they stand for and their name, I stripped the logo to the simplest form possible without losing the striking feeling the company was looking for.

The website follows the brand's core principles as well. The idea was to create a minimal, to the point site so new potential costumers would know exactly what the company offers.

The business cards are straightforward and sleek.



InkDrink

InkDrink is an online art store. Its team asked for a logo that related to their art style and represented their brand, all while keeping it simple and to the point. Illustrating their brand name in a literal sense was the solution, all while keeping the illustration in cool tones to reflect the usual palette used by the artist.


Web & UI/UX Design

FontDatabase.app

fontdatabase.app is a web application designed to help people find alternative fonts. Using an algorithm, it can help you find fonts more (or less) serious, detailed, formal, etc. to the one you input. Users can also help the algorithm grow by suggesting fonts themselves.

fontdatabase.app was featured in Product Hunt and international media outlets such as Periodismo.com, wwwhatsnew, and TekCrispy.

SoleMedia

I started designing and developing SoleMedia based on people's fixation with making the most out of their time using social media. The only apps that are remotely similar focused on content management, rather than timeline aggregation.

Since the time spent on social networks is, on average, quite long, I decided to go for a dark palette, making said time easier on the eye-

Making the sign in email-only (instead of requiring a SoleMedia account) proves to be more practical for the user, since it's one less user and password combination to remember. The interface aims to be as simple as possible, so as not to distract from the actual content.

M Tool Launcher

I designed The M Tool Launcher starting off the idea of a superapp, and why it never took off. Since people want to have a choice when it comes to each of their phone's features (messaging apps, social media, productivity tools, among other), a single app that does everything will never suit more than a couple individuals.

To solve this, The M Tool Launcher provides the benefits of acting like a superapp: reduces the time spend switching between apps, gives the experience of seamlessness navigation, all without sacrificing user choices.

Spur
